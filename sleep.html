Clinical Guidelines

The Science of Rest Protocol

schedule

Optimal Sleep Duration

6–7h

Minimum

7–9h

Optimal

>9h

Excess

Adults require 7–9 hours for full cognitive and physical recovery. Consistently sleeping under 6 hours increases cortisol, impairs glucose metabolism, and doubles cardiovascular risk over 10 years.

neurology

Sleep Architecture

N1

Light Sleep

Transition stage. Body temperature drops, muscles relax. 5% of total sleep.

N2

Core Sleep

Memory consolidation begins. Heart rate and breathing slow. 50% of total sleep.

N3

Deep Sleep (SWS)

Tissue repair, immune strengthening, glymphatic waste clearance. Most restorative — 20% of sleep.

REM

REM Sleep

Emotional processing, creativity, long-term memory encoding. Increases across the night — 25% of sleep.

bedtime

Pre-Sleep Protocol (60 min)

check_circle Dim lights 1 hour before bed — melatonin is light-sensitive
check_circle No screens 45 min before sleep (blue light suppresses melatonin by 50%)
check_circle Keep room at 65–68°F (18–20°C) — core temperature must drop to initiate sleep
check_circle No caffeine after 2PM — half-life of caffeine is 5–7 hours
check_circle Fixed wake time every day (±30 min) anchors your circadian clock
warning

When to Seek Help

Consult a sleep specialist if you experience: difficulty falling asleep for >30 min on 3+ nights/week, waking frequently, excessive daytime sleepiness, or if a partner reports snoring and pauses in breathing (possible sleep apnea).
